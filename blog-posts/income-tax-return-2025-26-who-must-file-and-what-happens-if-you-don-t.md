Every September, FBR sets a deadline for filing income tax returns. Every year, millions of people miss it — and spend the next twelve months paying more tax on every transaction they make. This guide explains who must file, what you actually need to do it, and why missing the deadline costs far more than most people realise.

## Who Is Required to File?

You must file an income tax return if any of the following apply to you:

- Your annual income exceeds **Rs 600,000** from any source
- You own immovable property with a land area of **500 square yards or more**
- You own a motor vehicle with engine capacity of **1000cc or more**
- You have a **National Tax Number (NTN)**
- You are a member of a **Chamber of Commerce** or a professional body
- You have paid electricity bills exceeding **Rs 500,000** in the year
- You have received a **foreign remittance** exceeding Rs 1 million in the year

If any one of these applies to you, you are legally required to file — regardless of whether you owe any tax or not.

## What Is the Deadline?

For Tax Year 2025-26 (July 1, 2025 to June 30, 2026), the deadline for individuals and AOPs is **September 30, 2026**. Companies with a June 30 year-end have until **October 31, 2026**.

## What Happens If You Don't File?

This is where most people get a nasty surprise. Missing the deadline does two things:

**First, you are removed from the Active Taxpayer List (ATL).** The ATL is FBR's list of people who have filed their returns on time. Being removed means you are classified as a "non-filer" for an entire year — even if you file late the next day.

**Second, non-filer withholding tax rates apply to everything you do.** Banks deduct more tax on your withdrawals and profits. Property purchases cost you more. Vehicle registrations become more expensive. The extra tax you pay across the year almost always exceeds whatever effort filing would have taken.

Here is a comparison of what you pay as a filer versus a non-filer:

| Trans\n\naction | Filer Rate | Non-Filer Rate |
| Bank cash withdrawal | 0% | 1.2% |
| Property purchase | 1.25% | 2.5% |
| Vehicle (above 1600cc) | 3% | 6% |
| Dividend income | 15% | 30% |
| Prize bond winnings | 15% | 25% |

If you buy one property in a year, the difference between filer and non-filer rates alone could cost you **hundreds of thousands of rupees**.

## What Do You Need to File?

For a salaried person, you need:

- Your CNIC number
- Your employer's salary certificate (Form 16 or equivalent letter)
- Bank statements showing profit or interest received
- Details of any property you own
- Details of any investments (National Savings, mutual funds, etc.)
- Your bank account number for any refund

For a business owner, you additionally need your business accounts showing profit and loss for the year.

## How to File

You file online through FBR's IRIS portal at **iris.fbr.gov.pk**. Log in with your NTN and IRIS password, navigate to Declaration → Income Tax Return → Tax Year 2025-26, complete all sections, and submit. You will receive an acknowledgement slip which you should save.

If this feels complicated, a tax consultant can file on your behalf — typically within 24 to 48 hours.

## The Bottom Line

Filing your income tax return is not optional if you meet the criteria above. More importantly, not filing is genuinely expensive. The ATL matters every time you do a banking transaction, buy a car, or purchase property. Spending an hour or two on your return — or hiring someone to do it — is one of the most financially sensible things you can do before September 30.
